Game Mechanics

Mega Evolution Mega Evolutions are legal in Regulation M-B and are now part of the core meta for the first time.
Timer Matches can now end in a draw when the 7-minute timer expires, replacing the previous HP-based tiebreaker.
Team Preview Team preview is set to 90 seconds and reveals both players’ Pokémon, including shinies.
Auto-Level All Pokémon are automatically adjusted to Level 50 for both Singles and Doubles play.
Rule Scope Regulation M-B expands the Champions roster with older generations while retaining core Champions gameplay.

New Pokémon

Notable additions Mega Raichu X, Mega Raichu Y, Mega Sceptile, Mega Blaziken, Mega Swampert, Mega Mawile, Mega Metagross, Mega Staraptor, Mega Scolipede, Mega Scrafty, Mega Eelektross, Annihilape, Gholdengo.
Classic returnees Vileplume, Qwilfish, Sceptile, Blaziken, Swampert, Mawile, Metagross, Staraptor, Musharna, Pyroar, Malamar, Dragalge, Falinks, Overqwil, Houndstone.

New Items

New item support Wide Lens, Muscle Band, Wise Glasses, Expert Belt, Light Clay, Life Orb, Zoom Lens, Metronome, Iron Ball, Icy Rock, Smooth Rock, Heat Rock, Damp Rock, Shed Shell, Big Root.
Item meta These additions broaden both offensive and utility builds, giving Champions teams more options for speed control, power boosts, and entry-hazard support.
